Profile

About

I am a Senior Researcher (Associate Professor level) at DTU, where I co-lead initiatives on data-centric energy systems within the Energy Economics and Modelling group. My work combines large-scale data platforms, machine learning, and behavioral insights to support utilities and policy makers with actionable intelligence.

I earned my PhD in Computer Science from Aalborg University (Daisy research group) and have held appointments at Åbo Akademi University, the University of Waterloo, and IBM Toronto Research. I collaborate widely with public authorities, industry partners, and interdisciplinary research teams to translate analytics into resilient energy planning tools.

Core Themes

  • Exploring AI robustness under information bottleneck constraints
  • Designing decision-support tools for industrial excess heat and flexibility markets
