Short Bio

I am a Senior researcher (Associate Professor level) at the Department of Technology, Management and Economics at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), where I am a member of the research group of Energy Economics and Modelling.

I completed my PhD at the Department of Computer Science (Daisy research group) at Aalborg University (AAU) in Denmark from 2008 to 2012. Before joining AAU, I worked as an Scientific Assistant at Åbo Akademi University in Finland from 2006 to 2008. From 2013 to 2014, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Data System Group at the University of Waterloo in Canada, and was also cross-appointed as a Research Scientist by IBM Toronto Research Center, where I worked on the soscip project.

Research Interests

  • My research interests include:
    • Smart meter data analytics
    • Data management, with a particular focus on data warehousing
    • Big data
    • Data mining & Machine learning
  • My current research projects include:
    • Benchmarking and evaluating smart meter data analytics systems and algorithms
    • Developing a smart meter data analytics system
    • Detecting anomalous energy consumption patterns
    • Investigating the intersection of energy informatics and big data management
    • Exploring the potential of the Internet of Things in these domains

Research Projects

OPTIX: Optimising Positive-Energy Districts through Interoperable Digital Platforms

01/12/2024-30/11/2027

Developing interoperable digital platforms to optimize positive-energy districts through advanced data analytics, energy modeling, and smart grid integration. The project aims to create sustainable urban environments that produce more energy than they consume.

Role: Co-PI. EU CETPartnership Project.

S3EM: A Semantic 3D Energy Model for Prosumer With Integrated Photovoltaics Systems

01/02/2025-31/01/2027

Creating a comprehensive semantic 3D energy model that enables prosumers to optimize their energy production and consumption with integrated photovoltaic systems. The project combines building information modeling, energy simulation, and semantic web technologies to create intelligent energy management solutions.

Role: PI. EU Marie Curie Project.

SciCloud: Scientific Cloud Computing Platform

01/01/2017-30/6/2018

Established a scalable private cloud computing infrastructure to support data-intensive scientific research at DTU. The platform provides researchers with on-demand computing resources, big data processing capabilities, and a centralized data lake for efficient data management and analysis across multiple research domains.

Role: Participant, responsible for cloud platform setup, management, and establishing data lake.

ANSWER: Advanced Network for Sustainable Wind-solar Energy Resource prediction
